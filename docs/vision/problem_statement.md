# Problem Statement

## The Market Gap

The children's care and developmental support industry in India is:

1. **Fragmented** — Therapists, educators, shadow teachers, and care providers operate in isolation with no shared discovery layer.
2. **Opaque** — Parents have no visibility into provider quality, session outcomes, or pricing structures.
3. **Transactional** — Most platforms facilitate a one-time discovery, not long-term care relationships.
4. **Clinically Intimidating** — Existing platforms use medical/therapeutic jargon that creates anxiety for parents who are just observing behavioral patterns in their children.
5. **Operationally Broken** — Providers manage bookings via WhatsApp, payments via UPI with no receipts, and notes via paper or personal notes apps.

---

## The Human Problem

> A parent notices their 6-year-old struggles to sit still, has meltdowns at transitions, and is falling behind in reading.  
> They search "therapist for child focus problems in Bangalore."  
> What they get: A 10-page list of clinics, insurance forms, and appointment waitlists.  
> What they needed: "Here are 3 providers who specialize in ADHD and focus issues, here's their approach, here's what parents say, here's a slot at 4 PM on Saturday."

---

## The Provider Problem

> Ananya is a certified special educator. She has 12 clients. She manages them via:  
> - WhatsApp for booking  
> - GPay for payment  
> - Notes app for session notes  
> - Google Calendar for availability  
> She has no business presence, no reviews, no recurring income stream, and no way to scale.
